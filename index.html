01 · Research

What we investigate

Our lab is built on a rare asset: direct, IRB-approved access to surgical patient samples including CSF, blood, and resected brain tissue. We leverage this to address unresolved questions in neurodegeneration, normal pressure hydrocephalus, and brain cancer through molecular and computational approaches.

Neurology

iNPH Biomarker Discovery

Idiopathic normal pressure hydrocephalus is a treatable yet under-diagnosed condition. We use patient-derived CSF and blood to discover molecular biomarkers that improve diagnosis, predict shunt responsiveness, and distinguish iNPH from other neurodegenerative diseases.

Neurodegeneration

Neurodegenerative Disease Mechanisms

We investigate the molecular overlap and divergence between iNPH, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and related conditions. Comparative multi-omics analysis of patient CSF and brain tissue aims to identify disease-specific molecular signatures.

Transcriptomics

Brain Tissue RNA-seq

Surgically resected brain tissue from iNPH, glioblastoma, and brain metastasis cases is profiled by bulk and single-cell RNA sequencing. We decode disease-specific gene expression landscapes in collaboration with electrophysiology experts at DGIST.

Oncology

Brain Cancer & Metastasis

We study the transcriptomic and molecular biology of glioblastoma and brain metastases from various primary cancers, using resected surgical specimens to characterise tumour heterogeneity and identify candidate therapeutic targets.

Evidence synthesis

Systematic Reviews & Meta-analyses

We conduct rigorous systematic reviews and meta-analyses to synthesise the global evidence base in hydrocephalus and iNPH, informing clinical practice and providing the evidence foundation for our experimental programmes.

Clinical biobank

Biobank & Multi-omics Platform

A prospective biobank of CSF, blood, and brain tissue from our PI's neurosurgical cases underpins all translational research. Samples are curated for proteomics, transcriptomics, and microplastic detection, with all collection conducted under approved IRB protocols.
